Pulse Detonation Engine Performance Analysis

❮ ❯

Objective

To investigate how combustion chamber length and fuel injection configurations affect the performance of Pulse Detonation Engines (PDEs), focusing on detonation dynamics and thrust production.

Methodology

A modular PDE system was constructed from Stainless Steel 304, featuring interchangeable combustion chambers. An Arduino-controlled dual-fuel injector system supplied Acetylene and Oxygen. A Shchelkin spiral facilitated the Deflagration-to-Detonation Transition (DDT), and thrust was measured using an S-type load cell at various frequencies.

Key Findings

A clear trade-off was identified: shorter chambers produced the highest thrust at lower frequencies. In contrast, the longest chamber demonstrated better stability and relatively higher thrust at the maximum frequency, making it more suitable for sustained high-speed operation.

CFD Analysis of a Dimpled Wing

❮ ❯

Project Overview

This project investigated the aerodynamic effects of adding dimples to a NACA 0018 airfoil using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD). A 3D CAD model was created in Creo Parametric, meshed, and analyzed in ANSYS Fluent under a 30 m/s airflow condition.

Key Findings

The study demonstrated that dimples delay boundary layer separation, reducing drag and enhancing lift at higher angles of attack. Results confirmed that dimples improve stall performance, fuel efficiency, and noise reduction, highlighting their potential in future aircraft design innovations.

Fabrication & Wind Tunnel Testing of a Dimpled Wing

❮ ❯

Project Overview

This project explored the aerodynamic benefits of dimples on a NACA 0018 airfoil through design, fabrication, and testing. A scaled dimpled wing was modeled in Creo, fabricated using a modified Ender 3 V2 3D printer with Klipper firmware, and post-processed for aerodynamic accuracy. PLA+ filament was used to ensure precision and durability.

Key Findings

Over 10 wind tunnel experiments were conducted to validate CFD simulations performed at 30 m/s airflow. Results confirmed that dimples delayed boundary layer separation, reduced drag, and enhanced lift, improving stall performance and aerodynamic efficiency. The combined CFD and experimental approach highlighted the potential of surface texturing in future aircraft designs.

Published Patent: Enhanced PDE Design

❮ ❯

Innovation Overview

This published patent introduces a novel Pulse Detonation Engine (PDE) design that enhances fuel-air mixing, stability, and detonation efficiency. The innovation employs a flat plate bluff body wake stabilizer to create controlled turbulence for improved combustion, along with perpendicular barriers in the Deflagration-to-Detonation Transition (DDT) chamber to accelerate flame propagation and ensure reliable detonation.

Impact & Application

This unique combination addresses inefficiencies in conventional PDEs, resulting in higher thrust-to-weight ratios, improved fuel efficiency, and more stable operation. The design has strong potential applications in both air-breathing propulsion systems and rocket engines, marking a significant step forward in detonation-based propulsion technology.

Research Paper: PDE vs. RDE Analysis

Paper Abstract

This research paper provides an in-depth systematic review and comparison of two cutting-edge detonation-based propulsion systems: Pulse Detonation Engines (PDEs) and Rotating Detonation Engines (RDEs). The study explores their fundamental principles, operational cycles, design constraints, and performance metrics.

Key Insights & Contribution

PDEs are highlighted for their simple design, while RDEs excel in continuous detonation for stability. The analysis identifies key advantages, such as reduced fuel consumption and higher specific impulse over traditional engines. This paper was presented at the International Conference on Sustainable Aerospace Technologies and Innovations 2025 (ICSATI-25) at VIT Bhopal, showcasing advancements in propulsion technology.
